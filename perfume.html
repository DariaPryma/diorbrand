DIOR's famous quote

“A woman's perfume tells more about her than her handwriting.”

In this section I will introduce the most popular DIOR's perfumes.

MISS DIOR

The first female perfume is "Miss Dior". Miss Dior was released in 1947 by Christian Dior.

The fragrance's name was made for the designer's sister - Catherine Dior, familiarly known as Miss Dior.

SAUVAGE

The first male perfume is "Sauvage". Sauvage was released in 1966 by Christian Dior.

The name of the fragrance is the result of Christian Dior's friend - Percy Savage because Dior had asked Savage to help find a name for a first perfume ever for men.

J'ADORE

The second female perfume is "J'adore". J'adore was released in 1999 by Christian Dior.

J'adore translate from the French for "I love".

Stages of notes in the perfumes

A fragrance note is a layer of scent. These layers are perceived at different stages of the fragrance's life on the skin.

In this table I'm going to show Top notes, Heart notes and Base notes of those three perfumes.

History of Perfumes

Perfumes were originally meant to emulate nature's pleasant aromas.

Natural oils were extracted from plants and became the main components of the perfumes. The oils were extracted, pressed, steamed and then burned in order to scent the surrounding air.

Early perfume was made using natural materials such as bark, wood, roots, leaves, flowers and seeds.
